Designing between the lines means looking past what’s in front of you and finding power in the unexpected. Into the Woods 2016 celebrates provocative ideas, integrative thinking, and boundary-defying design.

In April 2016, this legendary conference is back, with three days of keynote presentations, hands-on workshops and backwoods creative bonding at Sleeping Lady Mountain Resort in Leavenworth, Washington.

At the heart of Into the Woods is the opportunity to immerse yourself in an environment dedicated to helping you reconnect with your creative core. Designed to bring together top thinkers and practitioners from diverse fields to share ideas and experiences on everything from the design trends, the impact of design in today’s climate, to thinking about design in the future, and many other hot topics.

History of Into the Woods

For twenty years, Into the Woods has cultivated unexpected insights and meaningful connections between both people and ideas in a retreat style atmosphere.

In 1996, the very first Into the Woods was held at Lake Quinault Lodge, on the Olympic Peninsula in western Washington, and featured rustic atmosphere in the glacial-carved Quinault Valley. With a permanent move to the Sleeping Lady Mountain Resort, nestled in the Cascade Mountains in Leavenworth Washington, Into the Woods offers attendee’s majestic views and a cozy campus setting where we take over the campus for the entire 3-day event.

Past Into the Woods Keynote Speakers: Steve Frykholm, Yves Behar, Kyle Cooper, Stefen Fangmeier, Paula Scher, Seymour Chwast, Craig Frazier, Ellen Lupton, Angela Adams, Rick Valicenti, Gail Anderson, Matthew Carter, Kit Hinrichs, Allan Cobb, Kate Bingaman-Burt, Howard Lichter, Jay Ryan, and many more.

Founded in 1914, AIGA remains the oldest and largest professional membership organization for design. As design advocates, we bring together practitioners, enthusiasts, and patrons to amplify the voice of design and create the vision for a collective future. We define global standards and ethical practices, guide design education, inspire designers, and the public, enhance professional development, and make powerful tools and resources accessible to all.
